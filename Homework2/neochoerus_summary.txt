- Neochoerus is an extinct genus of rodent closely related to modern capybaras.
- The genus thrived during the Pleistocene epoch.
- Fossil remains of Neochoerus have been discovered in North America (Mexico and the United States) and South America (Colombia).
- Several species are attributed to this genus, including Neochoerus pinckneyi and Neochoerus aesopi.
- Neochoerus is part of the family Caviidae and the subfamily Hydrochoerinae.
