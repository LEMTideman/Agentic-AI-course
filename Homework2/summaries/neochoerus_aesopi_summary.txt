- **Neochoerus aesopi** is an extinct species of large rodent that lived in North America during the Pleistocene epoch, extinct around 12,000 years ago.
- It belongs to the subfamily **Hydrochoerinae** and is closely related to modern capybaras, weighing approximately 80 kg.
- Fossils have been discovered in several U.S. states, including Florida and South Carolina, illustrating its historical range.
- The species was first described in 1853 and has had various synonyms, including **Hydrochoerus holmesi**.
- Unlike surviving capybaras, **N. aesopi** originated from ancestors that migrated from South America during the Great American Interchange.
