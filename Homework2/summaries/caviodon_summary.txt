- **Caviodon** is an extinct genus of rodents that lived from the Late Miocene to Late Pliocene epochs, approximately 6.8 to 3 million years ago.
- It is part of the Caviidae family, which includes modern capybaras.
- Fossil remains have been discovered in Argentina, Venezuela, and Brazil.
- Known species include *C. andalhualensis*, *C. australis*, *C. cuyano*, *C. multiplicatus*, and *C. pozzii*.
- The genus was first described by paleontologist Florentino Ameghino in 1885.
